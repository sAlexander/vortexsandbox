Vortex Sandbox

What is a Vortex?

A vortex, in fluid dynamics, is a region of spinning fluid. Think of a tornado, hurricane, or draining bathtub.

The playground below simulates the movement of ideal, irrotational vortices. In these ideal, irrotational vortex, the speed of spinning fluid induced by a vortex is equal to 1/r, and the speed due to a collection of vortices is equal to the sum of each of those induced speeds.

How Do I use the Sandbox?

  1. Add a few vortices by clicking in the sandbox. A left click will add a positive vortex (counter-clockwise) and a right click will add a negative vortex (clockwise).
  2. Click Play!

Cool vortex examples:

  1. Blank Canvas - Creatively craft your own collection of vortices.
  2. Spinning Wheel - Notice how the spinning wheel slowly moves to the left due to the reflection off the solid wall.
  3. LeapFrog - The pairs of vortices continue to jump each other. Without a wall, this would continue forever! But with the wall, the leapfrog will slowly break apart.
  4. Expanding LeapFrog - The pairs of vortices jump each other, and as they approach the wall, the leapfrog width increases!
